Task1: Cine reconstruction

Background

Cine is the current gold standard for the noninvasive evaluation of cardiac functional parameters. Typically, cine MRI is obtained by retrospective electrocardiography (ECG) -gated segmented sequence since the full k-space cannot be obtained within one acquisition window, where the segments of the entire k-space are read out over multiple cardiac cycles. For patients with voluntary breath-holds or cardiac arrhythmia, image degradation will occur during the long acquisition window  because of motions, thus affecting the image quality. Accelerated cine MRI has the capability to address these limitations, in which the k-space of all temporal phases can be filled up in a less R-R interval and the cine images covering the entire left ventricle can be acquired in a single breath-hold. It is also possible to perform “real-time” imaging by accelerated reconstructions, therefore artifacts associated with respiratory motion and arrhythmia can be largely reduced. This challenge aims to provide a benchmark platform for the existing cine CMR reconstruction approaches to promote advances in this area of research.

Annotation

Radiologists' scoring according to the Likert score system will be performed on the op 5 participant groups in regard to the image overall the quality, artifacts, sharpness and contrast-to-noise ratio using the ground truth as the reference.

Data

Scanner: Siemens 3T MRI scanner (MAGNETOM Vida). 

CMR acquisition: We follow the recommendations of CMR exams reported in the previous publication (doi: 10.1007/s43657-02100018x, 10.1007/s43657-021-00018-x). We use the clinical sequence ‘TrueFISP’ for CINE imaging. The collected imaging planes include short-axis (SA) and two-chamber (2CH), three-chamber (3CH) and four-chamber(4CH) long-axis (LA) views. Typically 5~10 slices are acquired for  SA cine, while one slice is acquired for other views. The cardiac cycle is segmented into 15–25 phases with a temporal resolution 50 ms. For this challenge, we only provide raw k-space data of the SA and LA with all frames. Typical scan parameters: spatial resolution 2.0×2.0 mm2, slice thickness 8.0 mm, and slice gap 4.0 mm.

Pre-processing: T he raw k-space data exported from the scanner will be processed and transformed to HDF5 format. A readme file will be provided to describe the content and usage of the data. 

Metrics & Ranking

  • Metrics: PSNR, SSIM, RMSE and Radiologists' scoring
  • Ranking method: We will use a two-stage evaluation, where quantitative metrics will be used to select the top 5 submissions. These finalists will then be sent to radiologists to determine the winners. We use the SSIM as our quantitative image quality index for ranking submissions prior to submission to clinical radiologists. The final ranking will be decided based on the weighting of the quantitative metrics and the qualitative scoring. Participating teams are required to submit docker containers and process all the cases in the test set. For the cases without valid output, we will assign it to the lowest value of metric.
  • PSNR, SSIM and RMSE are commonly used metric and proven valid in previous challenges.
